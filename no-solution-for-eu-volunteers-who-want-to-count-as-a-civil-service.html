No solution for EU volunteers who want to count as a civil service

Christoph Kircher is a bit perplexed. The graduate actually came up with a plan early on: after completing his HTL in summer, he originally wanted to move to the Greek island of Chios. There he should have been involved in a project for almost a year aimed at strengthening the local community.
The 19-year-old wanted to do this as part of his community service. Or rather, he wanted to volunteer there as a civil replacement service in Austria. But now probably nothing will come of it. Because what Kircher planned and friends of him have recently implemented is no longer possible due to a pending amendment to the law.
Until autumn 2018, you could work as a civil servant as part of a European voluntary service abroad and the service was counted as a civil replacement service if it lasted for at least ten months. The background to this is that the projects have recently started under a different name at European level - European Solidarity Corps (ESK) - but Austrian law still speaks of the European voluntary service.
Already last December, those affected informed the then responsible Ministry of the Interior of the problem by open letter. This emphasized to examine the situation. Now the civil service agency (Zisa), which is now subordinate to the Ministry of Sustainability, announced on STANDARD request that the recognition of the ESK as a substitute for civil service was "to be seen critically". Because the possible uses "by no means offer the intensity and social performance for the candidates (...) as is the case with civilian or military service".
