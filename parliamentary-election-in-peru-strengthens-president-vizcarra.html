Parliamentary election in Peru strengthens President Vizcarra

Lima - The early parliamentary election in Peru has strengthened the position of impartial President Martín Vizcarra. Opposition leader Keiko Fujimori's right-wing populist Fuerza Popular, which had blocked Vizcarra's reform course, lost massive votes, as the Ipsos research institute announced on Sunday evening after 95 percent of the polling stations had been counted.
According to this, the Fuerza Popular only got 6.9 percent - in the previous parliamentary election in 2016 it had become the strongest with 36.3 percent. According to Ipsos, Fuerza Popular will be the sixth strongest in the new parliament with fewer than 20 seats. So far, the party has held a majority of 73 out of 130 seats. The official results were still pending.
Corruption allegations in the room
Keiko Fujimori, the daughter of former President Alberto Fujimori, is under pressure due to allegations of corruption in connection with the widespread affair surrounding the Brazilian construction group Odebrecht.
According to Ipsos, the moderately conservative Acción Popular with 10.1 percent will be the new strongest force in parliament, followed by the Christian fundamentalist movement Frepap with 8.8 percent. Vizcarra announced that he wanted to build a "responsible and mature relationship" with the new parliament, looking for a consensus in favor of the country.
Vizcarra dissolved parliament in September because the Fuerza Popular opposed its reforms head-on. Among other things, the head of state wants to use them to combat the widespread corruption in the South American country. According to surveys, 90 percent of Peruvians support his decision to dissolve parliament
