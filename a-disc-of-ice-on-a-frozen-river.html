A disc of ice on a frozen river

An impressive phenomenon of rare beauty occurred when a perfectly round disc of ice formed on the Soucook River in the New Hamsphire
In the last days, the perfect circle turned on itself thanks to the currents of the river. However, due to the cold, ice has started to form which now prevents it from moving.
This is the second time in two years that the phenomenon has occurred in the small municipality of Loudon.
"It's pretty cool. It's not something you see often, but now I've seen it twice in two years, "says Susan Aldrich.
Ice discs on a body of water only form a few times a year in the United States. A similar event occurred in the Gaspé in February 2019.
