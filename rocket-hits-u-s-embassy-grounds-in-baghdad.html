Rocket hits U.S. Embassy grounds in Baghdad

Baghdad - After several rocket attacks on US targets in Iraq, the site of the US embassy in the capital Baghdad has been hit directly. Prime Minister Adel Abdel Mahdi confirmed the attack and announced that Iraqi security forces would investigate the case. At least three people were injured, according to a report from the Al-Sumaria news site.
CNN broadcaster reported minor damage, citing a U.S. government official. There was initially no official response from the United States. The Iraqi military said that a total of five rockets had hit the highly secure government district on Sunday. At first, nobody confessed to the attack. Al-Sumaria, citing security circles, reported that helicopters would evacuate some people from the embassy site.
Several rockets had hit the city center of Baghdad in the past few weeks. Some of them landed in or near the government district, which houses the U.S. Embassy and the United Kingdom Embassy, among others. Attacks in this so-called Green Zone have also killed or injured people in recent years. Mostly there are local militias behind it, some of which are supported by neighboring Iran.
Abdel Mahdi condemned the recent attack, which "weakens the state and violates its sovereignty". It has "serious consequences for the whole country" and damages relations with allies, he said, according to the news agency INA.
