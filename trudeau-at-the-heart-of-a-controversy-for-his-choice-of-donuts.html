Trudeau at the heart of a controversy for his choice of ... donuts!

A seemingly innocuous photo showing Prime Minister Justin Trudeau buying donuts has gone viral and has been the subject of controversy on Twitter since Monday.
In the midst of a retreat with members of his caucus in Winnipeg, the Prime Minister decided to meet the sugar needs of his team by going to the local Oh Donuts pastry shop to stock up on donuts. In the photo shared on Twitter, we see the Prime Minister holding half a dozen boxes of sweet treats, apparently satisfied with his purchase.
For its part, the pastry shop highlighted the Prime Minister's visit. "We can confirm that he took them with him. We are almost certain that Health Canada will admit that everything is correct, in moderation ”, had fun trading on its own Twitter account.
It was enough for the passions of hundreds of Internet users to be unleashed. Under the Prime Minister's publication, more than 1,200 people expressed their thinking, often to criticize Justin Trudeau.
"I imagine that those of Tim [Hortons] are not good enough at $ 10 a dozen," rebelled a certain Bryan, in a message summarizing the popular fervor that took over the social network.
In comparison, Oh Donuts sells its regular donuts at $ 35 per dozen and its specialty donuts at $ 47 per dozen.
In the face of the outcry, the pastry shop made a point of clarifying things by affirming that it employs around thirty people benefiting from a pension plan and that it sources as much as possible with local products. "A Winnipeg Tim Hortons has locked out its employees for a 10-cent hour increase [and] their donuts are not fresh in all stores, but transported frozen by truck from an Ontario plant," said promotes trade to defend its prices.
