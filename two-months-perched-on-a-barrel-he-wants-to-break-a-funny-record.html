Two months perched on a barrel, he wants to break a funny record

Do you have to be ready to do anything to register your name in the Guinness Book of Records? Certainly, replied Vernon Kruger, who intends to leave his own there ... spending more than two months perched in a barrel hoisted 25m from the ground.
Far from the hiss that can be seen over the pages of the book, this 52-year-old South African nevertheless seems to have his feet firmly on the ground. Even underwater, he is a professional scuba diver.
And yet. Twenty-two years ago, Vernon Kruger established in his same city of Dullstroom (north-east) a new approved world record for "longest (human) stay perched in a barrel". 67 days and 14 minutes.
The fruit of chance, he remembers, tinged with a zest of bravado. “One day, I was on vacation on an island and I climbed on a palm tree. Someone said to me + you look very comfortable, why don't you try to break the record? ”
"At first it was a joke (...) and then it became a challenge to take up in front of my friends. And so I stayed perched for sixty-seven days. ”
Twenty-two years later, Guinness asked him to break his own record, still unbroken.
On November 14, Vernon Kruger was therefore hoisted in a 500-liter wine barrel placed on top of a 25m mast for a stay he knew, by experience, to be very spartan.
"Sleeping in a barrel is not very easy," he says from the top of his uncomfortable perch. "I've had a lot of cramps and back pain since I got up there. It is very narrow, I can only rest in the fetal position ”.
