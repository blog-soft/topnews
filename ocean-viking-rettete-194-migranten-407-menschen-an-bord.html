Ocean Viking rettete 194 Migranten, 407 Menschen an Bord

Rome - The rescue ship Ocean Viking received 184 people the night before Monday in Libya. They were on board two inflatable boats in distress. The first rescue operation took place off Libya, the second in Maltese waters.
In five rescue operations in less than 72 hours, Ocean Viking took a total of 407 migrants on board, the NGO SOS Mediterranee said on Twitter. Together with Doctors Without Borders (MSF) she runs the Ocean Viking.
The German rescue ship Alan Kurdi received 78 migrants on the Mediterranean off Libya on Saturday. The people were recovered in two different actions, said a spokesman for the aid organization Sea-Eye on Saturday. Several children are on board, the youngest six months old.
It is still unclear where the rescue ships will land. However, rescue ships do not currently have to wait too long on the Mediterranean before they are allowed to moor in Italy. Italy is making increasing efforts to redistribute the rescued migrants in Europe. Former right-wing Italian Minister of the Interior Matteo Salvini had completely prohibited rescue ships from entering Italian ports or only granted them after a long blockade at sea.
