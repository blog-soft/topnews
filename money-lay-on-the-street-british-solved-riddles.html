Money lay on the street: British solved riddles.

 

For years, stray bundles of money have kept a small town in north-east England in suspense. On Monday, the British police finally solved one of Blackhall Colliery's biggest riddles: two “good Samaritans” have been placing banknotes on the streets of the village since 2014 to help the poor and disadvantaged, officials from County Durham said.
Year after year, higher amounts of money had been found in the former mining town, none of which knew who they belonged to. The authorities were groping in the dark, fingerprints brought no information. Now an anonymous couple would have accepted the generous donations and contacted the police. "I am really glad that we have an answer to this riddle and that we can now definitely rule out that the money is connected to a crime or a person at risk," said Detective Commissioner John Forster.
Over the years, honest finders had given 13 bundles of money to the police with a total of more than 30,000 euros. How much money was distributed and not given away remains a secret of the "good Samaritans". According to police, the couple started depositing the money in sight in 2014 to make sure it was found.
Police Commissioner Forster urged people to continue to take the money found to the police first. He emphasized that all bundles of money found so far had been returned to the finders after a specified time.
