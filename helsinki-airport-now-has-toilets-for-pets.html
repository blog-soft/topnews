Helsinki Airport now has toilets for pets

Animals now have a quiet place at Helsinki Airport: Finnish airport operator Finavia has created two toilet areas for four-legged friends at Helsinki-Vantaa Airport. Two so-called Pet Relief Areas are designed to make traveling more comfortable for four-legged friends.
There have always been requests from passengers about where their pets can best relieve themselves on the premises, a Finavia spokeswoman said on Wednesday. The Finns' answer: initially two toilet rooms, called "Pet Relief Areas".
One of the two pet toilets is in the part of the airport where flights take off outside the Schengen area. Pictures show an artificial grass mat surrounded by a wooden fence. There is an artificial wooden stump on the mat for lifting legs. Another pet toilet was also created in front of the entrance to another departure lounge. Here, too, a green mat made of artificial turf was laid out in a corner of the toilet room and an artificial white tree stump was placed.
"Areas where pets can relieve themselves are still quite rare in northern European airports," said the spokeswoman. But the idea is not entirely new: In the United States, for example, such places are prescribed so that service animals from passengers - including guide dogs, for example - could be easier. According to the broadcaster, there are more than 10,000 dogs a year at Helsinki's airport. "We wanted to play a pioneering role in this area," said Hanna Hämäläinen, Head of Travel Services at Finavia, the public TV broadcaster YLE.
